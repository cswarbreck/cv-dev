#2Development

After a few years working in tech admin, I decided to learn how to code.

I started with the basics of HTML and CSS, and built a couple of (very) static sites. I struggled with responsiveness until I found Bootstrap, which also improved my use of JavaScript and JQuery. After that came dynamic AJAX apps built with NodeJS and I'm still learning how all the pieces of front-end JavaScript frameworks fit together.

#3Clients

While I've been developing my skills, I've worked freelance on a number of different projects.

My first commissioned site was for Tony Niccoli, which require('d') a lot of NodeJS. I also work for Julia Rose, developing content and planning her move to a full ecommerce business. As an artist, I've been commissioned by The Little Door & Co. to provide artwork for each location, which keeps creativity high.

#4.1Portfolio

Chess with Tony

Tony Niccoli is a chess teacher in London, running a small business. He needed a site that could be a focal point for delivering information, handle inquiries and would be easy to update. He didn't want to use Wordpress or an equivalent, so I built a site around KeystoneJS, an open source CMS using NodeJS with no on-going costs or tech support needed.

#4.2Portfolio

The Little Door & Co.

A new chain of upmarket concept bars, I was commissioned to paint the centerpiece for each location as they open. The first piece was delivered to the Little Blue Door in April 2018, and can be seen up top in #Welcome. The Little Yellow Door is soon to follow.
