#2Development

After working in tech for a couple of years, I decided to combine my creative and technical sides.

I started by building a couple of static sites in HTML/CSS. I then found Bootstrap to help with responsiveness, which also improved my use of JavaScript and JQuery. After that came dynamic NodeJS apps and I'm still learning how all the pieces of front-end JavaScript frameworks fit together. With these tools, there's no limit to potential creativity, and I'm always excited to add more.

#4.1Portfolio

Chess with Tony

Tony Niccoli is a chess teacher in London, running a small business. He needed a site that could be a focal point for delivering information, handle inquiries and would be easy to update. He didn't want to use Wordpress or an equivalent, so I built a site around KeystoneJS, an open source CMS using NodeJS with no on-going costs or tech support needed. The focus for me has been on the back-end and the site is still on the testing server, with Tony due to deliver the content by August 2018.
